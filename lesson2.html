🧠 Welcome, Future Coder!

Today, you are not just a student. You are a medical detective.

Your job? Read patient documentation carefully and uncover the correct diagnosis code using ICD-10.


🔍 What is ICD-10?

ICD-10 stands for International Classification of Diseases. It is the system used to translate diseases and health conditions into standardized diagnosis codes.

These codes help hospitals and insurance companies “speak the same language.”


🧩 Break the Code Structure

Example Code: E11.9

  • E → Endocrine diseases
  • 11 → Type 2 Diabetes
  • .9 → Without complications

See? Every character tells a story.


🏥 Real-World Scenario

A 55-year-old patient visits the clinic. The doctor documents:

“Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. Stable. No complications.”

🤔 What is the correct ICD-10 code?

Answer: E11.9

✔ Because it is Type 2 diabetes ✔ No complications mentioned


⚠️ Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Choosing unspecified codes too quickly
  • Ignoring laterality (left vs right)
  • Not checking full documentation
  • Forgetting secondary diagnoses

🎯 Mini Challenge

A patient is diagnosed with essential hypertension.

What is the ICD-10 code?
